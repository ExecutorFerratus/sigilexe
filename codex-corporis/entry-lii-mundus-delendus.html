The scream tore out of me before I could stop it—a raw, feral howl that ripped through the trees, echoing off the walls of the camp like something wounded and dying.

It was something dying.

Me.

Hazel Hanson was gone.

And in her place, again, stood Cassian Hale—a stranger wrapped around my bones, wearing my skin like armor I could never peel off.

I collapsed onto the ground, my fingers clawing at the dirt, desperate to hold onto something, anything that felt real, as if digging deep enough could bury the truth.

But the truth was carved into me.

I could feel it everywhere.

The broadness of my shoulders, the roughness of my hands, the flatness of my chest pressing against the ground. My voice—God, my voice—still raw from screaming, now carrying that deep, guttural edge that didn’t belong to me.

I punched the earth until my knuckles bled, gasping, sobbing, shaking.

"NO! NO! NO!" I roared, my voice breaking into something jagged and hollow.

Ren was beside me in an instant, her hands on my shoulders, trying to pull me back from the edge.

"Cassian—”

"DON’T CALL ME THAT!" I bellowed, twisting out of her grip, my face wet with tears and dirt. "I’m not him—I’m not—I’m—"

But I couldn’t finish.

Because even as the words fought their way out, my body betrayed me.

I was him.

No matter how much I screamed, no matter how much I denied it.

The women stood in a wide circle around me, some with pity in their eyes, others with cold indifference. Lira watched silently, her arms crossed, her expression sharp and distant.

"Look at me!" I shouted at her, my voice shaking with rage. "Is this what you wanted? To see me broken?"

She didn’t flinch. Didn’t move. Just stared.

"You think this is strength?" I spat, my voice trembling. "You think this body makes me less? It’s just flesh—it’s not who I am!"

THE MOMENT

Velius

[Cassian Hale (Hazel Hanson) POV]

"OH MY FUCKING GOD. NO!!!"

The scream ripped from my chest, raw and unfiltered, louder than anything I'd ever felt before—because I didn't just feel it, I WAS it.

"I WILL DESTROY THIS WHOLE FUCKING WORLD!!!"

I grabbed a fistful of dirt, hurling it into the air as if it could take my rage with it, but it just scattered like ashes—meaningless, like me.

The ground trembled beneath me, though whether it was real or just the trembling of my own body, I couldn’t tell. My fists pounded the earth again, harder this time, knuckles splitting open, blood mixing with dirt, grounding me in a body I wanted to crawl out of.

Ren tried to reach for me again, but I twisted away, staggering to my feet, breath ragged, chest heaving. My vision blurred with tears—or maybe fury, maybe both.

"DO YOU HEAR ME?!" I bellowed into the sky, as if the wizard could hear me through the void. "YOU THINK THIS IS A LESSON?! YOU THINK THIS IS JUSTICE?!"

The world felt too small for the rage inside me. My body couldn’t contain it, this prison of flesh and bone that wasn’t mine.

I wanted to tear it apart.

Rip it off.

Break it until there was nothing left of me to recognize.

"I HATE YOU!" I screamed, my voice cracking, echoing off the trees, off the stones, swallowed by a sky that didn’t care. "I HATE ALL OF YOU!"

The women stood in stunned silence, their faces blurred through the haze of my tears. Some with pity. Some with fear. Lira, of course, with that same unreadable, stone-cold face.

"You did this!" I spat, pointing at all of them, at no one, at everyone. "You made me believe I was nothing the second I became him. You treated me like I wasn’t even a person!"

My knees buckled, and I collapsed again, fists pounding the ground until I couldn’t feel my hands anymore—until the pain was loud enough to drown out everything else.

"I want to die," I whispered, face pressed into the dirt. "I’d rather die than live like this."

The words hung there, fragile and ugly, because they were true.

I felt Ren’s hand on my back, trembling.

"Cassian…"

"DON’T CALL ME THAT!"

I rolled onto my back, staring up at the sky, wishing it would swallow me whole.

But it didn’t.

Because life is cruel like that.

It lets you break.

It lets you scream.

But it doesn’t let you escape.

Not like this.
